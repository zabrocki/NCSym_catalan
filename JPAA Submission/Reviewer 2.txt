Reviewer 2: I cannot tell (without a more careful reading of this paper and a couple of references cited in it) how far beyond Cartier-Milnor-Moore the results really go. The assumptions are stronger so it is reasonable that they do.

I would also like to know how the results of Sections 5 and 6 interact with the results of Lagrange's Theorem for Hopf Monoids in Species, Canadian Journal of Mathematics 65 (2013), no. 2, 241-265. This paper is not cited. Even if the results are not directly comparable, a reference to this paper seems warranted, since they are of a similar nature: both sets of results provide necessary conditions on the graded dimensions for the existence of surjections or embeddings of graded connected Hopf algebras. In the case of the above citation, the results are more general as they apply without the assumptions of cocommutativity or freeness. For the same reason, the results of the current paper may provide stronger conditions. I would like this to be discussed in the paper.
